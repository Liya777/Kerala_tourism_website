Top Destinations

Ramakkalmedu

Ramakkalmedu is a tiny hamlet in the Idukki district. The scenic beauty of Ramakkalmedu has turned it into a fantastic tourist place with thousands of people visiting it. From here, one can get beautiful views of the sunrise in the eastern hills and the sunset on the eastern mountains.

Cheeyappara Waterfalls

One of the largest waterfalls in the state, the Cheyyappara Falls are a sight to behold. Cascading through seven tiers of stones, these falls are a force of nature that you can not only see and experience but dip your toes into and enjoy.

Meesapulimala

The second highest peak in Southern India, Meesapulimala stands elevated at an altitude of 2641 meters above sea level. The densely covered terrain has eight peaks that together resemble a moustache and is, therefore, called Meesapulimala. Meesapulimala has been recognised as a UNESCO world heritage site.


Periyar National Park

The Periyar National Park in Thekkady, Kerala, is one of the most bio-diverse regions in the world and the best-protected reserve area that one can lay eyes on in India. Famous for its gorgeousness, greenery and stillness, the park is the dwelling place of abundant significant species.

Idukki Arch Dam

Idukki Arch Dam, an engineering marvel. It is an arch dam with a double curvature and is built on River Periyar. Precisely, the dam is constructed in a gorge between two hills, Kuravathi and Kuravan. It is at the height of about 550 feet and is a breathtaking sight in the otherwise rugged terrain along which has been built.

Hill View Park

Located just about a kilometre from the Idukki city centre, the Hill View Park is set up approximately 350 feet from the Idukki dam. As the name suggests, Hill View Park is one such places in Idukki that offers spectacular views of the Cheruthoni and Idukki Dams.
