Top Destinations

Chandragiri

Chandragiri also known by the name Payaswini, is the largest river in Kasaragod district, state of Kerala, India. It was named after the great Mauryan king Chandragupta Maurya. It is believed that he visited the river on his way to Shravanabelagola.

Bekal Fort

Bekal Fort was built by Shivappa Nayaka of Keladi in 1650 AD, at Bekal. It is the largest fort in Kerala, spreading over 40 acres.

Edayilakkad

Edayilakkad, is a marshy island situated in Kasaragod district, Kerala, India. It is located in the Kavvayi Backwaters. A small forest is there in Edayilakkad. It is the shelter for many monkeys.


Ranipuram Hills

Ranipuram is a renowned tourist destination in the northern tip of Kerala. Located in Kasaragod, it is situated 750 m above sea level. It makes for a perfect picnic spot where one can even come across the occasional herd of elephants.

Bela Church

Bela Church, also Our Lady of Sorrows Church, is a Roman Catholic church in the Kasargode district of Kerala state, 15 km north of Kasaragod. It was built in 1890 and is thus the oldest church in the district.

Kottancheri Hills

Kottancheri Hills is an eccentric hill station located at a height of 152 meters above sea level, near Malom town in Kasaragod district, Kerala, India. Encircled with the Western Ghats, the Kottancheri Hills even extends to the Ranipuram Wildlife Sanctuary.
